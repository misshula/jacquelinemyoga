about

Jacqueline is a Vancouverite transplanted in Toronto with a love of yoga, gluten-free goodies, painted toenails, public libraries, un-tucked hotel sheets, sunshine, heart-opening back bends, Old Spice, and epic walks around the city. While managing both a corporate job and teaching yoga, she finds balance on and off her mat.


Jacqueline discovered yoga at a retreat in Greece in 2007 where she fell in love with the practice. She hasn’t looked back since. After trekking all over the world with her mat slung over her shoulder, she landed in the wonderful city of Toronto. Jackie completed her teacher training at Kula Annex and is grateful for her teachers Christi-an Slomka and Lawrence-Jacob Milman. She has also been fortunate to study with Ryan Leier, Dina Tsouluhas, and Dustin Fruson. She will forever be a student of yoga, searching out formal training, while learning the most from her students and her personal practice.


Jacqueline keeps her yoga classes fun-filled so her students can feel comfortable approaching their yoga practice with a sense of curiosity, compassion, freedom, playfulness, and courage. Weaving creativity into each class, she looks and finds the beauty in everyone. She likes to kick your ass, but kiss your soul.


She also loves to play music – you’ll be guaranteed to hear some Lauren Hill, Madonna, or Florence + the Machine.
